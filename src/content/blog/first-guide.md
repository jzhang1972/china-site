## Why Mobile Payments Matter

China is one of the world's most cashless societies. Whether you're buying a coffee, taking a taxi, shopping at a mall, or ordering street food, you'll almost always pay by scanning a QR code.

Although cash (Chinese Yuan, RMB) is still legal and merchants are generally required to accept it, most people rarely use cash anymore. Many small businesses may not have enough change or simply expect customers to pay digitally.

For the smoothest travel experience, it's highly recommended to set up **Alipay** and **WeChat Pay** before you arrive.

---

## Which Payment App Should You Use?

For most international travelers, installing **both** apps is the best option.

| App | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Alipay (支付宝)** | Shopping, restaurants, taxis, attractions, train tickets, convenience stores, food delivery, and everyday purchases. |
| **WeChat Pay (微信支付)** | A backup payment method and shops that prefer WeChat Pay. Some merchants only display WeChat QR codes. |

Most travelers use Alipay more frequently, but having both apps ensures you can pay almost anywhere.

---

## Before You Travel

Before boarding your flight to China, make sure you have:

- A valid passport
- An international credit or debit card (Visa, Mastercard, JCB, Discover, etc.)
- An active mobile phone number that can receive SMS verification codes
- Internet access during setup
- Both payment apps installed

If possible, link **two different bank cards** in case one card doesn't work.

---

# Setting Up Alipay

Alipay is the easiest payment app for most foreign visitors.

### Step 1

Download **Alipay** from the App Store or Google Play.

### Step 2

Sign up using your international mobile phone number.

### Step 3

Verify the SMS code.

### Step 4

Go to:

**Me → Bank Cards → Add Card**

### Step 5

Add your international credit or debit card.

### Step 6

Complete identity verification if requested.

You may be asked to upload your passport for security verification.

### Step 7

Your bank may ask you to approve a small verification transaction.

Once completed, you're ready to pay.

---

# Setting Up WeChat Pay

WeChat Pay works similarly.

### Step 1

Download **WeChat**.

### Step 2

Create an account using your international phone number.

### Step 3

Open:

**Me → Services (or WeChat Pay) → Wallet → Cards → Add Card**

(The menu may look slightly different depending on your app version.)

### Step 4

Add your bank card.

### Step 5

Complete verification if required.

## How QR Code Payments Work

China mainly uses two types of QR code payments.

### Option 1: You Scan the Merchant's QR Code

This is common in restaurants, cafés, and small shops.

Steps:

1. Open Alipay or WeChat Pay.
2. Tap **Scan**.
3. Scan the merchant's QR code.
4. Enter the amount if required.
5. Confirm payment.

---

### Option 2: The Merchant Scans Your QR Code

This is common in supermarkets, convenience stores, and shopping malls.

Steps:

1. Open the app.
2. Tap **Pay**.
3. Show your payment QR code.
4. The cashier scans your code.
5. Payment is completed automatically.

## Do You Still Need Cash?

Yes—but only as a backup.

We recommend carrying **¥200–500 RMB** in cash for emergencies.

Cash may still be useful if:

- Your phone battery dies
- Your internet connection isn't working
- You're visiting remote rural areas
- A payment system is temporarily unavailable

For most travelers, you'll rarely need to use cash.

## Fees You Should Know About

### Bank Fees

Some banks charge international transaction fees (typically around 1–3%).

Check with your bank before traveling.

### Alipay & WeChat Pay Service Fee

For international bank cards, payments above certain amounts (for example, over **¥200 RMB**) may incur a small service fee.

Fee policies can change, so always check the latest information inside the app.

## Common Problems

### My payment was declined.

Possible reasons include:

- Your bank blocked the transaction.
- Your card doesn't support overseas mobile wallet payments.
- Identity verification hasn't been completed.
- Your internet connection is unstable.
- The merchant only accepts domestic payment methods for certain services.

---

### I can't receive the verification SMS.

Make sure:

- International roaming is enabled.
- Your phone has signal.
- Your mobile carrier allows international SMS.

---

### The app is in Chinese.

Both Alipay and WeChat support English.

Open **Settings → Language** and switch to English.

---

### Can I use Apple Pay or Google Pay?

Only at some merchants that accept international contactless cards.

They are **not** a replacement for Alipay or WeChat Pay in China.

---

### Can I get a refund?

Yes.

Refunds are usually returned to the original bank card linked to your account.

Depending on your bank, the refund may take several business days to appear.

---

### Is it safe to link my bank card?

Yes.

Both Alipay and WeChat Pay use advanced encryption and are trusted by hundreds of millions of users.

For additional security:

- Enable biometric login (Face ID or fingerprint)
- Use a strong password
- Never share verification codes
